Rate & Term Refinance

Rate and term refinancing allows you to replace your current mortgage with a new one at a lower interest rate or with a different loan term. This is the most common type of refinance and does not involve taking cash out of your home.

Benefits

  • Lower monthly mortgage payment
  • Reduce total interest paid over time
  • Shorten your loan term (e.g., 30 to 15 years)
  • Switch from an adjustable-rate to a fixed-rate loan

Considerations

  • Closing costs typically apply
  • You need sufficient home equity
  • The rate must be low enough to justify costs

Cash-Out Refinance

Cash-out refinancing lets you replace your current mortgage with a larger loan and receive the difference in cash. This is useful if you want to access your home’s equity for renovations, debt consolidation, education, or other major expenses.

Benefits

  • Access home equity as cash
  • Fund home improvements or renovations
  • Consolidate high-interest debt
  • Potentially lower your overall interest rate

Considerations

  • Increases your total loan balance
  • May result in higher monthly payments
  • Requires more home equity

Frequently Asked Questions About Refinancing in Nevada

What’s the difference between rate & term and cash-out refinancing?
Rate & term refinancing changes your interest rate or loan length without taking cash out. Cash-out refinancing gives you cash by increasing your loan balance.
When should I consider a cash-out refinance?
Cash-out refinancing is often used for home renovations, debt consolidation, education expenses, or other large financial needs.
How much can I cash out when refinancing in Nevada?
Most lenders allow you to cash out up to 80% of your home’s appraised value, though this can vary.
Do I have to pay closing costs when refinancing?
Yes. However, many borrowers roll closing costs into the new loan or choose a no-closing-cost refinance option.
